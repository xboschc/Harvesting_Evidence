Why this website?

This website:

 

  • is structured around 'topics' to facilitate the application of findings for decision making and implementation
  • uses a common framework (the WHO building blocks)
  • summarises the best available evidence on the effects of interventions related to health systems and/or immunisation
  • provides a platform for the participation of policy makers, managers, technical staff and the public in general
  • looks at all levels of detail, from key messages drawn from reviews, to specific studies and data

 

Too often research articles end without definitive conclusions and call for further research. Yet policy makers and managers still need to make decisions. Evidence needs to be appraised and correctly interpreted, but should not become and endless process. Poverty and disease is widespread and will not wait for us to make decisions. Furthermore, resources are limited and it is of foremost importance to use them efficiently, within the context of a sound priority setting.

 

This website is structured around topics. It provides evidence on specific topics related to health systems and/or immunisation. It 'harvests" local and global evidence, of diverse quality and strength, making it accessible to policy makers, managers, technical personnel and the public in general.

 

Each topic has a key message contributed to by more than one review in many cases. Evidence from systematic reviews are the basis of the key messages, which  take into account evidence from particular settings, experiences on the implementation of interventions, and prospects for further innovation. All of this should have a formal place within this website. Therefore, the key messages and evidence provided are not the 'last word': they rely on the efforts of the international health community to improve, complete, and trim the contents, integrating it into a structured and accessible format for different types and levels of evidence. It is only by providing a common place for all interested parties that knowledge can be built.

 

This website also provides an additional insight into the studies (experimental or not) that have been part of the reviews or that contain potentially relevant information to understand the reviews and to provide hints on how interventions have been implemented in specific contexts. By making the individual studies available to the users of this site, specific examples, experiences or cases can more easily be examined as inspirational sources of what can be done to address a problem.

 

In other words, different types of information from different sources will be consolidated around each topic to produce and illustrate a key message that can inform policy. Furthermore, a table summarises all interventions and outcomes included in this website to show at a glance what has been researched and included here, and what works and what does not.

 

GAVI is committed to strengthening health systems offering an explicit support line to which countries can apply. This website also aims at assisting countries to identify viable solutions to their health systems and immunisation programmes and get the most from both national and international support.

How to navigate the site.

The backbone of this website are the topics.

 

Topics are classified within the building blocks of the WHO health systems framework (in the upper left menu). 

 

Topics for which there is no content yet are not shown.

 

For each topic there are three levels of detail, which can be navigated using the icons in the upper right column.

  • The first icon represents the KEY MESSAGE level for that topic, providing a recommendation useful for managers and decision makers.
  • The second icon represents the REVIEWS level, where the evidence supporting the key message is provided.
  • The third and more detailed level represents the STUDIES level, where information on the relevant studies included in those reviews is provided.

 

An overview of topics available is in the sitemap.

 

There is a fourth level of detail in a series of pages and tables, useful for those who wish to have a more comprehensive overview of what is included in this site so far.

 

  • To find out how strong the conclusions of a review are: detailed information of reviews is provided, including the geographical distribution of relevant studies and the evidence profile of each intervention (one review may address several interventions). For example: Would this intervention be applicable in my setting?
  • To find out the magnitude of the effects: see the table showing all interventions and their effects on outcomes grouped by category, that allow an easy comparison of what issues have been researched and with which results. For example: What is the magnitude of the effects I can expect?
  • To track the studies by intervention and outcome: there is a table showing which studies in each review have addressed which interventions and outcomes. This is useful to identify specific studies that could be of interest to a particular user. For example: Is there a study specially relevant for what I am looking for?
  • To see a description of reviews and studies: go to the detailed table of features of reviews and studies, including objectives, participants, interventions, outcomes, heath system setting, among others. For example: What areas have been researched so far?

 

We have also included additional contents. This is information may not be directly related to any particular topic, but can be useful to understand the topics, or could be otherwise relevant to your work. This additional information will be enhanced in time, based on users preferences.

 

  • Immunisation: a series of resources on immunisation from representative institutions dealing with the issue and describing the main resources available on the Web.
  • Policies and guidelines: a collection of the main policies related to immunisation and 'how to' documents potentially useful to the visitors to this website.
  • Global health data: links to find data related to health, in the forms of reports, datasets or interactive online websites.

 

See the methods section for more details.

Contribute!

There are several ways to contribute to this website. This site was actually conceived for this purpose. It is a community effort to bring together the best available global and local evidence related to interventions to improve immunisation and other health related outcomes.

 

If you don't have much time:

  • send us a quick email, a short sentence, whatever you think could help us improve.

 

If you wish to help us understand the profile of users and give a structured opinion:

  • please, fill in the questionnaire, it is also quite quick.

 

If you would like to engage in a discussion, or provide technical advice on the contents of the site:

  • the forum is your place. For full access please register and we will moderate as effectively as possible.
