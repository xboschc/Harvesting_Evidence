WHO

Type: Database

 

Scope: All countries

 

Indicators: More than 100 indicators: e.g. Mortality and Disease Burden, Morbidity, Life expectancy, Health service coverage, Risk Factors, Health workforce, demographic and socioeconomic indicators, regional and country data, inequities

 

Brief Description: WHOSIS, the WHO Statistical Information System, is an interactive database bringing together core health statistics for the 193 WHO Member States. It comprises more than 100 indicators, which can be accessed by way of a quick search, by major categories, or through user-defined tables. The data can be further filtered, tabulated, charted and downloaded. The data are also published annually in the World Health Statistics Report released in May

WHO

Type: Database

 

Scope: WHO Regional Office in Africa, Americas, Eastern Mediterranean, Europe, South-East Asia, Western Pacific

 

Indicators: Mortality and Disease Burden, Morbidity, Life expectancy, Health service coverage, Risk Factors, Health workforce, demographic and socioeconomic indicators, regional and country data, inequities

 

Brief Description: Health indicators compiled and maintained by WHO regional offices. WHO common indicators can be searched by country of the specific region and per year. Outputs comprise data tables and often also maps, graphics or reports. Each regional database has it's own structure and focus areas but most important health information can be accessed by country.

WHO

Type: Database

 

Scope: All countries

 

Indicators: All indicators related to chronic diseases: e.g. alcohol consumption, BMI, tobacco use, cholesterol etc.

 

Brief Description: The WHO Global InfoBase is a data warehouse that collects, stores and displays information on chronic diseases and their risk factors for all WHO member states. It is a database which allows to extract information on all selected chronic diseases per country, the outputs provided consist of a list of surveys conducted in that particular field in that particular country with links to data. Data can be searched by country, topic or indicator, a comparison between countries is possible. A glossary and FAQ page is also available.

 

Link: WHO
