The Diseases of the Most Impoverished (DOMI) Program

Vaccine Development

Vaccine Development:

 

  • Description of the IVI DOMI (Diseases of Most Impoverished) Program focusing on research and development of vaccines against three important enteric diseases - cholera, shigellosis, and typhoid fever.
  • Pediatric Dengue Vaccine Initiative (PDVI) targetting the formation of strategic Partnerships, reserach and development, vaccine evaluation and access
  • Japanese Encephalitis (JE) Program including various surveillance studies, long-term effectiveness studies as well as cost-effecitveness studies, analyses of JE policies
  • The IVI's Respiratory Pathogen Vaccines Program focuses on diseases due to Haemophilus influenzae type b (Hib), meningococcus, pneumococcus and influenza. This program is working to provide better estimates of the disease burden of childhood pneumonia, meningitis and sepsis due to these pathogens.
  • IVI surveillance program on Rotavirus
  • Cholera Vaccine Initiative ("CHOVI". The aim of this Initiative is to control cholera by developing and deploying safe and effective oral cholera vaccines in populations at risk for endemic or epidemic cholera.
  • IVI Vi-based Vaccines for Asia (VIVA) Initiative. The aim is to ensure a cost-competitive supply of Vi polysaccharide vaccine for introduction in high-risk typhoid-endemic areas.
  • Laboratory Sciences dedicated to vaccine research, development, technical assistance and technology transfer.
