Immunization in General

WHO publishes a series of regularly updated position papers on vaccines and vaccine combinations against diseases that have an international public health impact.

 

These papers are concerned primarily with the use of vaccines in the global or regional context and with large scale immunization of specific high risk groups. The position papers summarize essential background information on the respective diseases and vaccines, and conclude with the current WHO position concerning their use in the global context.


WHO recommendations for routine immunisation  to assist programme managers to develop optimal immunization schedules WHO has compiled basic information on its current routine immunization recommendations into two summary tables.


WHO catalogue of immunisation policy recommendations are available according to disease, special interest and recommendations from the advisory committee.


Links exist to strategic advisory groups on immunization: e.g. Strategic Advisory Group of Experts (SAGE) on Immunization, which guides the work of the WHO Immunization Department.

 

Global Immunisation Vision and Strategy (GIVS): UNICEF and WHO organisation aiming
at assisting countries to vaccinate more people.


WHO list of pre-qualified vaccines: A list of UN pre-qualified vaccines with links to some of the manufacturers can be accessed.

 

Global Immunization country and regional statistics/data: Annual data available per country,
region, per subject through WHO/UNICEF joint reporting; Immunization schedules per country/region and antigen.


WHO immunisation highlights e.g. a document describing global trends in immunization,
WHOs competencies, achievements in (research, policy, regions) in 2006/07 and providing
 information on Global Immunization News (GIN).
